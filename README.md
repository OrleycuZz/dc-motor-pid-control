## 🧠 Technical Description

### 🔌 Electrical Dynamics
The DC motor armature circuit is modeled using Kirchhoff’s voltage law:

V = R·i + L·(di/dt) + e

where:
- V is the applied voltage
- R and L represent the armature resistance and inductance
- e = Ke·ω is the back electromotive force (EMF), proportional to motor speed

This equation captures the transient and steady-state behavior of the electrical subsystem.

---

### ⚙️ Mechanical Dynamics
The mechanical behavior of the motor is governed by:

J·(dω/dt) + b·ω = T

where:
- J is the rotor inertia
- b is the viscous friction coefficient
- T = Kt·i is the electromagnetic torque generated by the motor

This describes how the motor accelerates and reacts to load and friction.

---

### 🔄 Electromechanical Coupling
The electrical and mechanical domains are coupled through:
- Torque generation: T = Kt·i
- Back EMF: e = Ke·ω

This coupling introduces a natural feedback effect:
as speed increases → back EMF increases → current decreases → torque reduces

---

### 📉 Transfer Function Model
By combining electrical and mechanical equations, the motor can be represented as a transfer function:

ω(s)/V(s)

This model is used for controller design and analysis in the frequency domain.

---

### 🔁 Closed-Loop Control System
A feedback control loop is implemented where:
- The output speed ω is measured
- Compared to a reference ω_ref
- The error is processed by a PID controller

The controller adjusts the input voltage to minimize the tracking error.

---

### 🎯 PID Controller Role
The PID controller improves system performance by:

- Proportional (P): reduces rise time and reacts to error magnitude  
- Integral (I): eliminates steady-state error  
- Derivative (D): improves damping and reduces overshoot  

Proper tuning ensures stability, fast response, and robustness.

---

### 🔬 Simscape vs Transfer Function Model
Two modeling approaches are used:

- Transfer Function:
  - Simplified mathematical model
  - Useful for control design and analysis

- Simscape:
  - Physical modeling approach
  - Captures real-world nonlinearities and interactions
  - Closer to real system behavior

Comparing both models allows validation of assumptions and controller performance.
